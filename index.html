Knowledge Base

What is CGPA?

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is a measure of a student's overall academic performance, calculated by averaging grade points across all subjects over a specific period. Unlike a single semester's GPA, CGPA reflects your academic consistency and dedication throughout your entire program — making it one of the most important metrics universities, employers, and scholarship committees use to evaluate students.

Most universities worldwide use a 4.0 scale, where each letter grade (A, B, C, etc.) corresponds to a specific grade point value. Your CGPA is a weighted average of these values across every course and every semester you have completed.

Calculation Method

CGPA Formula

The CGPA formula takes into account both the grade you earned and the number of credits each course carries. Higher-credit courses have more influence on your final CGPA than lower-credit ones.

CGPA =
∑ (Total Grade Points in a Semester × Credits) Total Credits Attempted across all Semesters

For example, if you earned a 4.00 in a 3-credit course and a 3.00 in a 2-credit course, your GPA for those courses would be: (4.00×3 + 3.00×2) ÷ (3+2) = 18 ÷ 5 = 3.60. This same logic is applied cumulatively across all semesters.

Comparison

CGPA vs GPA

Students often confuse CGPA and GPA — while they are closely related, they measure different things. Here's a clear breakdown of the key differences:

Aspect GPA (Grade Point Average) CGPA (Cumulative GPA)
ScopeSingle semester or termEntire academic program
Also Known AsSGPA (Semester GPA)Overall / Cumulative GPA
Calculation PeriodOne term at a timeAll completed semesters
Use CaseTracking semester performanceScholarships, jobs, grad school
Resets Each Semester?Yes — fresh each termNo — running average
Impact of Bad SemesterOnly affects that semesterLowers your overall CGPA
Impact of Good SemesterDoesn't fix past semestersGradually improves CGPA

In simple terms: GPA tells you how you did this semester, while CGPA tells you how you've done throughout your entire degree. Both matter — a strong CGPA requires consistent performance across all semesters, not just a few good ones.
