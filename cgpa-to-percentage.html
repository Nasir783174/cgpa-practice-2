CGPA to Percentage: Use a CGPA Calculator or Do It Yourself - Here Is How

Published on: 21/03/2026

Whether you use a CGPA Calculator or prefer working it out by hand, understanding the conversion from CGPA to percentage is an essential skill for every student. Job applications, scholarship forms, visa documents, and university admission portals almost always ask for a percentage - even if your university only gives you a CGPA.

The tricky part is that there is no single universal formula. The conversion depends on which grading scale your institution uses - 4.0, 10.0, 5.0, or another. Use the wrong formula and you could report a percentage that is higher or lower than your actual standing.

This guide covers every major grading system in clear, plain language, with worked examples for each one so you can convert your CGPA confidently and accurately.

Why Is There Not Just One Simple Formula?

This is a question almost every student asks. The answer comes down to how grades are assigned in the first place.

On a 10-point scale, a grade of 10 does not necessarily mean you scored 100% on every exam. Universities typically use grade bands - for example, scoring between 90% and 100% in your exams might all be graded as a 10. Because of this compression, you cannot just multiply by 10 and call it a percentage. You need a specific multiplier that accounts for this adjustment.

On a 4.0 scale, the same principle applies. A 4.0 GPA does not mean you scored 100% in everything. The scale simply assigns grade points to letter grades, and the relationship between those points and raw percentages is not perfectly linear.

📌 Key Takeaway: CGPA scales compress raw percentage marks into grade point ranges. Each conversion formula is designed to undo that compression and give an approximate percentage equivalent.

Formula 1 - The 10-Point Scale (CBSE Standard)

This is the most widely used conversion formula in India and is recognised by many international credential evaluation services. It uses a multiplier of 9.5.

Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

Why 9.5 and Not 10?

The reason for using 9.5 instead of 10 is that a CGPA of 10 on the 10-point scale represents the highest possible grade band - not a perfect 100% in raw marks. The 9.5 multiplier is calibrated to reflect that reality more accurately.

Think of it this way: in most universities, even a student who scores 95% or above in their exams receives a grade of 10. So multiplying by 9.5 instead of 10 keeps the conversion honest and realistic.

Worked Examples - 10-Point Scale (CBSE)

  • CGPA of 6.0 → 6.0 × 9.5 = 57.0%
  • CGPA of 7.5 → 7.5 × 9.5 = 71.25%
  • CGPA of 8.4 → 8.4 × 9.5 = 79.8%
  • CGPA of 9.2 → 9.2 × 9.5 = 87.4%

✅ Quick Rule: For CBSE and most standard 10-point scale universities, multiply your CGPA by 9.5 and you have your percentage.

Formula 2 - The 4.0 Scale (US and Global Standard)

On a 4.0 scale, there is no single official percentage conversion that every institution agrees on. However, the most widely used method - and the one used by major credential evaluators like WES (World Education Services) - is a simple proportional formula.

Percentage = (CGPA ÷ 4.0) × 100

Worked Examples - 4.0 Scale

  • CGPA of 2.5 → (2.5 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 62.5%
  • CGPA of 3.0 → (3.0 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 75.0%
  • CGPA of 3.6 → (3.6 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 90.0%
  • CGPA of 3.9 → (3.9 ÷ 4.0) × 100 = 97.5%

⚠️ Important: This formula is a linear estimate. Different US universities may use slightly different percentage bands for the same grade point. Always check with the specific institution or evaluator.

Formula 3 - AICTE Standard (Technical Education in India)

The All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) governs engineering, management, and other technical programmes across India. For these programmes, AICTE uses a slightly different formula:

Percentage = (CGPA - 0.75) × 10

Worked Examples - AICTE

  • CGPA of 7.0 → (7.0 - 0.75) × 10 = 62.5%
  • CGPA of 8.0 → (8.0 - 0.75) × 10 = 72.5%
  • CGPA of 8.75 → (8.75 - 0.75) × 10 = 80.0%
  • CGPA of 9.5 → (9.5 - 0.75) × 10 = 87.5%

📌 Note: If you studied engineering, pharmacy, MBA, or another AICTE-regulated programme in India, use this formula rather than the standard CBSE 9.5 multiplier.

Critical Steps Before Using Any Conversion for Official Purposes

  1. Check your official transcript for the exact authorized formula.
  2. Follow the instructions from the recipient (university/job portal).
  3. Do not round up unless instructed; round to two decimals.
  4. Use credential evaluators like WES for international applications.
  5. Contact your university registrar if in doubt for official conversion.

💼 Pro Tip: For high-stakes applications like graduate school or skilled migration visas, always get an official document. A small error can delay or reject your application.

Common Mistakes Students Make When Converting CGPA

  • Multiplying every CGPA by 9.5 regardless of scale.
  • Assuming CGPA × 10 is always correct.
  • Using the same formula for all programmes - AICTE vs CBSE matters.

Quick Summary - All Formulas at a Glance

  • 10-Point Scale (CBSE / Standard): Percentage = CGPA × 9.5
  • 4.0 Scale (US / Global): Percentage = (CGPA ÷ 4.0) × 100
  • AICTE (Technical India): Percentage = (CGPA - 0.75) × 10
  • European ECTS: Based on cohort ranking, not a multiplier
  • Canada / Australia: Use institutional lookup tables

🎓 Final Thought: Converting CGPA to percentage sounds simple, but doing it correctly takes care. Use the right formula, follow instructions, and get an official document when needed. A small difference can make a big impact.
